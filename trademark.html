TRADEMARK FILING

Trademark filing is the first step in protecting a brands identity. It involves submitting an application with the relevant trademark office to secure exclusive rights over a name, logo, symbol, or tagline associated with a business. Proper classification and documentation ensure a smooth registration process.

Go to Section

EXAMINATION REPLY

Once a trademark application is reviewed by the examiner, objections may arise based on similarity, descriptiveness, or procedural issues. An Examination Reply involves drafting a legally sound response addressing the concerns raised to ensure successful trademark approval.

OBJECTION HEARING

If an examination reply does not fully satisfy the authorities, an Objection Hearing is scheduled where legal arguments are presented before the registrar to justify the trademark's distinctiveness and eligibility for registration.

OPPOSITION/COUNTER REPLY

After a trademark is published in the journal, third parties can oppose its registration. We assist in filing an Opposition Reply to defend the mark and, if needed, counter an opponents claims with strong legal reasoning and evidence.

EVIDENCE SUBMISSION

Supporting evidence is crucial in opposition cases or hearings. This involves submitting documents like prior usage records, brand recognition data, and sales reports to strengthen the case and establish ownership rights.

OPPOSITION HEARING

An Opposition Hearing is conducted when a trademark faces legal challenges. Our team represents clients before the trademark authorities, presenting arguments and evidence to secure the approval or defend the trademark.

TRADEMARK WATCH

Trademark watch services help businesses monitor potential infringements by detecting similar or conflicting marks in official registries. Regular monitoring prevents unauthorized usage and helps in taking timely legal action.

TRADEMARK RENEWAL

A trademark is valid for a specified period (usually 10 years) and requires timely renewal to maintain exclusive rights. We assist in Trademark Renewal to prevent lapses and protect brand ownership.

C&D NOTICES AND REPLY

Cease & Desist (C&D) Notices are legal warnings issued to individuals or businesses using a trademark without authorization. We assist in drafting and responding to such notices, ensuring rightful protection and resolution of disputes.

What is the difference between Trademark, Copyright, Patent, and Design Protection?
Trademark: Protects brand names, logos, and slogans. Copyright: Protects original literary, artistic, musical, and software works. Patent: Protects new inventions and technological innovations. Design Protection: Safeguards the visual appearance of a product, such as its shape, pattern, or color.
How long is the validity of a Trademark, Copyright, Patent, and Design Registration?
Trademark: 10 years (renewable indefinitely). Copyright: Lifetime of the creator + 60 years (varies by country). Patent: 20 years (non-renewable). Design Registration: 10 years (extendable for 5 more years)
Can I apply for international protection for my Trademark, Patent, or Design?
Yes, international protection is available: Trademark: Use the Madrid Protocol for global registration. Patent: Apply via the Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT). Design: Use the Hague System for international design registration. Copyright: Automatically recognized in many countries under the Berne Convention.
What happens if someone infringes on my Trademark, Copyright, Patent, or Design?
You can take legal action by: Sending a Cease & Desist notice. Filing an opposition or objection with the IP authority. Pursuing a lawsuit for damages and seeking an injunction against the infringer.
